Rules

Rules:

  • Two Options: The focus of the game is giving players two distinct possibilities. These choices have to be equally appealing or challenging and may be humorous, challenging, or thought-provoking.
  • Taking Turns: Players take turns being presented with two alternatives in a group environment. It may be arranged randomly or in a circle.
  • Making a Choice: The person whose turn it is must select one of the two alternatives that are given. Their decision is final, and if they wish, they should provide justification.
  • Discussion and Debate: Following the player's selection, the group may hold a discussion or debate regarding the available choices. This frequently sparks interesting discussions and exposes more about the interests and priorities of each member.
  • No Opting Out: Players cannot refuse to respond or opt out; they must make a decision even if both possibilities are unattractive.
  • Respectful Play: The game should be played with good humor and respect. Avoid asking any potentially harmful or disrespectful questions.
  • Winning or Score: The game "Would You Rather" isn't normally one where there is a winner or score. Instead of maintaining score, the emphasis is on having fun and talking to others.

NOTE: Keep the game interesting by offering a variety of options, ranging from humorous to more serious, and tailor the questions to the group's preferences and comfort level.
